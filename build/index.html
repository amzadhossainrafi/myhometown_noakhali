About Noakhali

Noakhali is a coastal district in southeastern Bangladesh, known for its lush agriculture, intricate network of rivers and canals, and beautiful islands like Hatiya and Nijhum Dwip.

The district boasts a unique cultural identity characterized by a peaceful lifestyle, renowned hospitality, and a distinct local dialect that reflects its rich heritage.

Top Attractions

Bajra Shahi Mosque

This 18th-century heritage mosque features stunning ornate mosaics and stands as a testament to the region's architectural heritage.

Nijhum Dwip

An eco-tourism paradise known for its mangrove forests, spotted deer, and diverse bird species.

Meghna River

Experience serene boat rides and witness the vibrant fishing culture along this mighty river.

Charbata

This quiet island area in Hatiya offers peaceful retreats amidst natural beauty.

Historical Legacy

Noakhali was part of the ancient Samatata region and gained historical significance during the 1946 communal riots known as the "Noakhali riots".

Despite these challenges, the region has maintained a strong tradition of cultural and religious tolerance. It evolved from colonial zamindar rule to become an important agricultural and trade hub in independent Bangladesh.

The district is also notable as the birthplace of Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani, a prominent political leader.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

November to February offers the most pleasant weather with cool and dry conditions perfect for exploration.

How to Get There

From Dhaka, you can travel by road or take a launch to Hatiya. Regular bus services connect the capital to Noakhali town.

Where to Stay

Options range from local guesthouses in Nijhum Dwip to comfortable hotels in Noakhali town.

What to Eat

Don't miss the local shutki (dried fish), traditional pithas, and delicious fish curry.
